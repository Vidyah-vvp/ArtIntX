Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)
Navy SEAL technique for instant calm. Activates parasympathetic nervous system. Tap to expand.

How to do it:
  1. Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 counts
  2. Hold your breath for 4 counts
  3. Exhale slowly through your mouth for 4 counts
  4. Hold empty for 4 counts. Repeat 4‚Äì6 times.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding
Sensory grounding technique for anxiety and dissociation. Brings you into the present.

Focus your attention on each sense, one at a time:

  • 5 things you can SEE ‚Äî Look around the room
  • 4 things you can TOUCH ‚Äî Feel different textures
  • 3 things you can HEAR ‚Äî Listen carefully
  • 2 things you can SMELL ‚Äî Any scents nearby?
  • 1 thing you can TASTE ‚Äî Your mouth right now

This technique interrupts the anxiety feedback loop by anchoring your awareness in physical reality.

Thought Record (CBT)
Challenge cognitive distortions by examining the evidence for and against your thoughts.

When you notice a distressing thought, work through these questions:

  • What is the automatic thought? Write it exactly as it appeared.
  • What is the emotion and its intensity (0‚Äì100%)?
  • What evidence supports this thought?
  • What evidence contradicts this thought?
  • What would you tell a close friend with this thought?
  • Write a balanced alternative thought and re-rate the emotion.

Behavioral Activation
Break the depression-inactivity cycle by scheduling meaningful activities.

Depression reduces motivation and pleasure. Behavioral Activation reverses this.

  • Create a list of activities you used to enjoy or that give a sense of accomplishment
  • Rate each activity for pleasure and mastery (0‚Äì10)
  • Schedule at least one activity every day, starting small
  • Do the activity even if you don't feel like it ‚Äî mood follows action
  • Record how you actually felt during and after ‚Äî it's usually better than predicted

Gratitude Practice
Shifts attention patterns. Proven to increase well-being within 2 weeks of daily practice.

Daily gratitude practice rewires the brain's attention toward positive stimuli over time.

  • Every evening, write down 3 specific things you're grateful for
  • Be specific ‚Äî not "family" but "my sister called to check on me today"
  • Write about why each thing matters to you
  • Savour the feeling for 20‚Äì30 seconds on each item
  • Over time, try to notice good things in real time throughout your day

Opposite Action (DBT)
From Dialectical Behavior Therapy ‚Äî act opposite to the urge driven by a difficult emotion.

When an emotion urges you toward an unhelpful behavior, do the opposite:

  • Identify the emotion and its action urge (e.g., fear ‚Üí avoid)
  • Check if the emotion is justified and the action is effective
  • If not, identify the opposite action (e.g., fear ‚Üí approach)
  • Act opposite all the way, including body posture and facial expression
  • Repeat until the emotion shifts

How Depression Affects the Brain

Depression involves changes in neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine), neuroinflammation, and reduced hippocampal neurogenesis. Treatment reverses these changes.

üíä

How Antidepressants Work

SSRIs increase serotonin availability. SNRIs target serotonin and norepinephrine. Effects typically emerge after 2-6 weeks. Work best combined with therapy.

üèÉ

Exercise as Antidepressant

150 min/week of aerobic exercise is as effective as SSRIs for mild-to-moderate depression. Exercise increases BDNF, serotonin, and endocannabinoids.
